About

How this tracker works — and how to read its retirement math.
What is this?

A personal finance tracker built as a single HTML file — no account, no server, no subscription. Everything runs in your browser and saves to localStorage. Export a JSON backup anytime; load it back in any browser to pick up where you left off.

The focus is on net worth growth over time and two retirement planning frameworks: Traditional FIRE — when can you retire? — and Coast FIRE — when can you stop contributing?

Recommended Workflow
1
Add your accounts on the Accounts tab — checking, savings, 401(k), brokerage, debts, etc.
2
Set your name, birth year, and salary on the Settings tab.
3
Enter your first balance snapshot via + Add Month Snapshot on the Dashboard.
4
Configure your retirement targets on the FIRE or Coast FIRE tabs.
5
Export a Save JSON backup after every monthly entry and store it somewhere safe.

What is Traditional FIRE?

FIRE — Financial Independence, Retire Early — is built on one idea: once your portfolio is large enough to fund your lifestyle indefinitely via investment returns, you can stop working. The FIRE tab answers: at what age does that happen?

The FIRE Number
Annual spending ÷ Safe Withdrawal Rate. At a 4% SWR you need 25× your yearly expenses. Spending $60,000/year means a $1,500,000 FIRE number.
Tax Adjustment
If your accounts are mostly pre-tax (401k, Traditional IRA), withdrawals will be taxed. Enter an effective tax rate and the FIRE number is grossed up — you need more than 25× take-home spend to actually cover your lifestyle after taxes.
How the simulation works
The app grows each account month-by-month at its own return rate, adding contributions and employer match each month. It stops the clock the first month your total crosses the FIRE number — that's your earliest FIRE age.
How to Use the FIRE Tab
1
Set your monthly spend
What you expect to spend per month in retirement, in today's dollars.
2
Configure SWR and average return
4% SWR and 7% return are common starting points. Lower SWR = larger, safer target. Higher return = faster growth.
3
Set an effective tax rate (optional)
Mostly Roth? Use 0–5%. Mostly traditional pre-tax? Use 20–30%. Mixed? 10–20%.
4
Mark accounts for retirement
On the Accounts tab, check "Include in retirement calculations" for each account that counts. Exclude short-term buckets like emergency funds.
5
Read your earliest FIRE age
The chart is blue while contributing, turns green when the portfolio crosses the FIRE number. The stat at the top shows your earliest retirement age.
6
Save a Plan Snapshot
Lock in today's projection. Revisit months later — the snapshot compares your planned trajectory against actual balances and tells you if you're on track.

What is Coast FIRE?

Coast FIRE asks a different question: at what age can you stop contributing to your portfolio and let compounding alone carry it to your full FIRE number by retirement?

Once you've hit your coast number, you no longer need to save — you just need to cover living expenses. Many people use this milestone to shift to part-time work, a lower-stress career, or passion projects.

The Coast Number
FIRE Number ÷ (1 + return rate)years until retirement. The further you are from retirement, the smaller the coast number — compounding does proportionally more work.
Coast vs. Traditional FIRE
Reaching Coast FIRE does not mean you retire. You still need income to cover expenses from coast age to retirement age. The freedom is that income no longer needs to include savings — just living costs.
How the simulation works
Each month, the app computes the coast number for that future age (FIRE number discounted back by remaining years) and checks whether your current portfolio has crossed it. The first month it does is your Coast FIRE age.
How to Use the Coast FIRE Tab
1
Set your target retirement age
The age you want to fully retire — this is the end point compounding grows toward. Common choices: 55, 60, or 65.
2
Enter monthly spend and SWR
Same inputs as the FIRE tab — these define your full FIRE number, which Coast FIRE works backward from.
3
Set your expected return and tax rate
The return rate during coasting is the key lever — a higher assumed return means a smaller coast number is needed today.
4
Read your Coast FIRE age
The chart is blue while contributing and gold during the coasting phase. The stat at the top shows the age you can stop contributing.
5
Save a Plan Snapshot
Lock in today's numbers. Return months later to see if you're ahead or behind — the snapshot shows planned vs. actual portfolio and recalculates your coast age.

What happens between age 38 and 60?
You still need to cover living expenses for 22 years — but now your income only needs to cover day-to-day costs, not retirement savings. A part-time job, freelance work, or a low-stress career becomes financially viable well before traditional retirement.
